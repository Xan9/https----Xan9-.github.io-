The Seven Types of Sea Turtles

Loggerhead Turtles

Adult Loggerheads weigh 250 pounds and can be up to three feet in length, hatchlings weigh 0.05 pounds and are about 2 inches in length.
The lifespan of these turtles is unknown but they reach sexual maturity at around 35 years old. These turtles eat whelks and conch.
Females nest from April to September and usually lay 3 to 5 nests per season.
Nowadays most loggerheads live on Masirah Island, Oman, elsewhere in the Middle East, and on the coast of the southeastern United States.

Green Turtle

Adult green turtles weigh 300 to 350 pounds and are generally three feet in length, hatchlings weigh 0.05 pounds and generally are 2 inches in length.
The lifespan of this turtle is unknown. These turtles eat seagrasses and algae.
Females return to the same beaches where they were born every 2-4 years to lay eggs, they generally do this in the summer months.
Green turtles nest on the east coast of Florida, Surinam, and Tortuguero,Costa Rica.

Leatherback turtles

Adult Leatherbacks weigh up to 2,000 pounds and are generally 6.5 feet in length, hatchlings weigh 1.5 to 2 ounces and are from 2 to 3 inches in length.
Their lifespan is unknown. These turtles eat soft-bodied animals including jellyfish, salps, and pyrosomes.
There has been a recent increase in leatherback nesting on the central and south eastern coast of Florida but all of their major nesting beaches in the pacific are declining.
Females lay clutches of about 100 eggs many times during a nesting season, typically at 8 to 12 day intervals.

FLatback Turtles

Adult flatbacks weigh an average of 198 pounds and measure up to 3.25 feet in length.
Their lifespan is unkown but is estimates at around 80 years old. This turtle eats sea cucumbers, jellyfish, mollusks, prawns, bryozoans, other invertebrates and seaweed.
These turtles inhabit coastal coral reef and grassy shallows and is only found in the northern coastal area of Australia and the Gulf of Papua New Guinea.

Hawksbill

Adult Hawksbills weigh from 100 to 150 pounds and measure from 25 to 35 inches, hatchlings weigh 0.5 ounces.
Their lifespan is around 30 to 50 years. They eat sponges and other invertebrates, algae.
Females return to the beaches where they were born to nest, this occurs every 2-3 years at night and approximately every 14 to 16 days during the nesting season.
Their only stable populations are in Yemen, northeastern Australia, the Red Sea, and Oman.

Kemp's Ridley Turtle

Adult Kemp's Ridley turtles weigh 100 pounds and measure from 24 to 28 inches, hatchlings weigh 0.5 ounces and measure 1.5 inches.
Their lifespan is unkown. They eat crabs,fish,jellyfish, and mollusks.
These turtles use "arribada" nesting where large groups gather and come ashore and nest all at once.
The only major breeding site of the Kemp's ridley is on a small strip of beach at Rancho Nuevo, Mexico.

Olive Ridley Turtles

Adult Olive Ridley turtles weigh 100 pounds and measure from 22 to 31 inches, hatchlings weigh less than one ounce and measure 1.5 inches.
Their lifespan is unknown, but they reach sexual maturity at about 15 years. They eat algae, lobster, crabs, tunicates, mollusks, shrimp, and fish
Females nest every year, once or twice in a season, laying clutches of approximately 100 eggs.
Large arribadas of Olive Ridleys still occur in Pacific Costa Rica, primarily at Nancite and Ostional and Pacific Mexico at La Escobilla, Oaxaca.
